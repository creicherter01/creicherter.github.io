Who I Am

Design that's human at heart

I specialise in simplifying complex workflows and building intuitive interfaces that users love. With a background in user research and interaction design, I bring a rare lens to the work: one shaped by psychology.

I believe that great design isn't just beautiful — it's functional, inclusive, and human-centered. Having a background in Psychology assists me not just in the research phase of design but in the design phase as well, by tailoring my thought process to user needs.

My approach combines rigorous user research, systematic design thinking, and iterative refinement to create solutions that truly resonate with people. Every pixel, every interaction, every flow is intentional and purposeful.

My philosophy

What fascinates me most is how small, intentional design decisions can dramatically transform how people interact with a product.

A single button placement, a clearer label, or a simplified flow can mean the difference between frustration and delight. That's what drives me every day.

My Background

My Journey to UX Design

From a psychology degree to pixels — an honest account of how I found the field that lets me do what I love most: understand people.

01
The Starting Point
A psychology degree and an open question

After graduating with my degree in psychology, I found myself at a crossroads. While I've always been drawn to understanding people and their behaviors, the traditional psychology career paths didn't resonate with me. I knew I wanted to apply my deep understanding of human psychology in a way that felt innovative and impactful.

02
The Discovery
Where psychology and technology intersect

My research led me to UX design — a field where psychology and technology meet. I realised that understanding user behavior, motivations, and pain points is at the heart of creating meaningful digital experiences. Every design decision should be rooted in human psychology and user research. That realisation sparked my passion for the field.

03
The Honest Part
Not a "tech person" — and that's my advantage

Learning design software felt daunting at first. But I discovered that my psychology background gave me a unique edge. While others focused on aesthetics and technical execution, I was asking deeper questions: Why does this interaction feel confusing? What mental model do users have? How can we reduce cognitive load? These questions became my north star.

04
The Commitment
Human needs at the centre of innovation

As technology continues to evolve at an accelerating pace, I'm committed to ensuring that human needs remain at the centre of innovation. That means asking harder questions, listening more carefully, and designing with both rigour and empathy — every single time.
