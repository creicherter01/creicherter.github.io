About this book
Piranesi's house is no ordinary building: its rooms are infinite, its corridors are filled with endless marble statues, and its staircases plunge into the roaring sea. Piranesi lives there alone — with the birds, the tides, and the clouds that roll through its halls — and keeps meticulous journals of his explorations. One day, a stranger appears and asks for Piranesi's help. What follows is a mystery both haunting and wondrous.
